**No-medical-exam life insurance** can be a practical path for people with **pre-existing conditions**—but it is not magic. These products trade a blood draw and paramedical exam for **health questions** (and sometimes prescription and MIB checks). You still must qualify based on how you answer and what carriers allow.

## Types you may hear about

- **Simplified issue** — Short health questionnaire; no exam. Approval may be quick, but not everyone qualifies.
- **Graded or modified benefit** — May include a waiting period before the full death benefit pays for non-accidental death; sometimes easier to approve for serious health histories.
- **Guaranteed issue** — No health questions (within limits); typically lower face amounts and graded benefits. Often used for final expense when other options are not available.

## Who it helps

People with **stable, well-managed** COPD, AFib on appropriate therapy, controlled diabetes, or history of CAD treated per guidelines *may* fit certain carriers—**subject to underwriting**. There is **no universal yes** for “no exam + any condition.”
