**Taking Eliquis (apixaban)** usually means you are managing **stroke risk**, often because of **atrial fibrillation** or a history of clots. Insurers are familiar with Eliquis; it is **not automatically a decline**. Underwriting cares about **why** you take it, **how stable** your condition is, and your **overall health**.

## Why medication-based ads work

Many people search **life insurance on Eliquis** before they search “AFib life insurance.” If that is you, you are still in the right place—the medication is a **clue** to your risk profile, not the whole story.

## What you should have ready

- Date of AFib diagnosis (if applicable)  
- Last cardiology visit  
- Other heart history (stents, CHF, etc.)  
- Any recent hospitalizations  

## Xarelto and Warfarin

The same ideas apply to **Xarelto** and **warfarin**. Carriers want **compliance** and **stability**, not “no prescriptions.”

## No guarantee of approval

Eliquis alone does not qualify or disqualify you—**underwriting looks at the full picture**.
