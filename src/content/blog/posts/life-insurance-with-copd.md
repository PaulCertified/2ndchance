**Can you get life insurance if you have COPD?** In many cases, **yes**—especially when your condition is **stable**, you follow your treatment plan, and you apply for products whose guidelines fit chronic lung disease. COPD includes emphysema and chronic bronchitis; underwriting often looks at stage, medications, oxygen use, and recent exacerbations.

## What carriers may review

- **Spiriva (tiotropium), Symbicort, Advair, albuterol**, and other inhalers—documented as prescribed.
- **Oxygen therapy** — If you use supplemental oxygen, options may be more limited, but some carriers still offer graded or simplified products.
- **Recent hospitalizations or ER visits** for COPD flares—timing matters.

## No-exam and simplified options

Many shoppers search for **life insurance with COPD and no medical exam**. Simplified-issue policies can fit **mild to moderate**, stable COPD for some carriers. **Approval and pricing are never guaranteed**; they depend on your full health picture.

## Do not give up after one “no”

If you were **declined for life insurance because of COPD**, another carrier or product type may still work. This is the core of **second-chance** underwriting help.
