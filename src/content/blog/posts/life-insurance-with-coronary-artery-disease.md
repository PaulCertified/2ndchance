**Coronary artery disease (CAD)**—narrowed or blocked arteries supplying the heart—affects millions of Americans. Life insurance underwriting focuses on **how severe** your disease is, **what was done** (stents, bypass), **how long ago**, and how you are doing **today**.

## Typical medications

**Atorvastatin** or other statins, **aspirin**, **clopidogrel (Plavix)**, and **nitroglycerin** are common. Underwriters use your history and follow-up—not the prescription list alone—to assess risk.

## Stents and bypass

Many people qualify after successful **PCI (stent)** or **CABG** once a stable period passes. Recent heart attack or unstable angina usually means waiting or choosing different product types.

## No-exam options

Shoppers often ask about **life insurance with heart disease and no medical exam**. Simplified products may work for **stable** CAD in some cases; others need fully underwritten offers or graded plans. **Nothing here is a guarantee.**

## Second chances after a decline

If you were **declined for life insurance due to CAD**, do not assume every carrier will agree. Guidelines differ.
