If you are **between 50 and 75** and have **health issues**, you may still have meaningful life insurance choices. This age band often overlaps with **mortgage balances**, **peak earning years**, or planning for **final expenses**—and carriers know many applicants take medications or live with chronic conditions.

## What makes this ICP unique

- You may have been **declined years ago** when your health was different—or when you applied for the wrong product.  
- You may identify more with **medications** (e.g., blood pressure pills, inhalers, anticoagulants) than with a formal diagnosis name.  
- You want **clarity fast**—which is why simplified and no-exam paths are popular when they fit.

## Product types to discuss with an agent

- **Term** (if you qualify and need temporary protection)  
- **Permanent** or **whole life** (for lifetime needs or certain impaired-risk markets)  
- **Final expense / burial** (smaller face amounts, often simplified or graded)  

## No guarantees

Age and health do not automatically disqualify you—but they also do not **guarantee** approval. Underwriting rules apply.
