**Mortgage protection** generally means life insurance structured so that, if you pass away, your family can **pay off or keep paying** the home loan. If you have **heart or lung conditions**, prior declines, or take prescriptions like **Eliquis** or **Spiriva**, you might assume you cannot qualify—but **many people can still obtain coverage** depending on product and carrier.

## How it differs from “mortgage insurance” from the lender

Lender-offered products often pay the **bank**, not your family, and may not give you the flexibility of an individually owned policy. **Term or permanent life insurance** you own can be structured to match your mortgage balance and term—**talk to a licensed agent** about what fits your state and loan.

## Health conditions and underwriting

Stable **COPD**, **AFib**, **CHF**, **CAD**, and other diagnoses do not automatically remove you from the market. You may see **standard**, **rated**, or **graded** offers—or need a specialized carrier.

## Emotional angle: protecting the family home

For many shoppers, this is not about a product name—it is about **“If something happens to me, my spouse can stay in the house.”** That is a legitimate planning goal; the right policy depends on facts and underwriting.
