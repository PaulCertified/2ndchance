**Congestive heart failure (CHF)**—when the heart does not pump blood as efficiently as it should—is taken seriously by life insurance underwriters. That does **not** mean coverage is impossible. Many people with **stable, treated** CHF qualify for **graded**, **simplified**, or certain **permanent** products depending on ejection fraction, symptoms, and recent events.

## Medications underwriters may ask about

Common therapies include **lisinopril** or **losartan**, **carvedilol** or **metoprolol**, and **furosemide (Lasix)**. Having these prescriptions is normal for CHF; carriers care more about **control**, **follow-up**, and **stability** than the names alone.

## What improves your chances

- Regular cardiology follow-up  
- Clear diagnosis and treatment plan in your records  
- Time since last hospitalization for heart failure  
- Honest answers on the application
