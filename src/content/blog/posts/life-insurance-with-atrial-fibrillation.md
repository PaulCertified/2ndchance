**Atrial fibrillation (AFib)** is common, and many people with AFib can obtain life insurance—especially when rhythm or rate is **controlled** and stroke risk is **managed** (often with anticoagulants). Underwriting will usually review your **type of AFib**, **treatments**, and **overall cardiac history**.

## Blood thinners: Eliquis, Xarelto, Warfarin

Taking **Eliquis (apixaban)**, **Xarelto (rivaroxaban)**, or **warfarin** for stroke prevention is **expected** in AFib care. Carriers generally want to see **compliance** and **stability**, not absence of medication. **Amiodarone** or other antiarrhythmics may prompt extra questions.

## Why some people are declined

Recent ablation complications, uncontrolled rate, new clots, or combined severe heart disease can narrow options. That is when **alternative carriers** or **simplified / graded** products matter.
