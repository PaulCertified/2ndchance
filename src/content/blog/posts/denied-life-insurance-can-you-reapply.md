**Yes, you can often reapply** after being denied life insurance—but timing, honesty, and product choice matter. Reapplying the next day with the same carrier and the same product type rarely helps if nothing has changed. A smarter approach is to understand *why* you were declined and then look for a **better match**.

## When reapplying makes sense

- Your health has **stabilized** or enough time has passed since a surgery, hospital stay, or new diagnosis.
- You originally applied for a product with **strict underwriting** (full exam, full records) and may qualify for **simplified** or **graded** coverage instead.
- The decline was due to **missing information** that you can now provide.

## When to wait

If you were declined for a **recent** major event, some carriers want a period of stability before they will reconsider. Your agent can explain typical waiting periods for your situation—this is general education, not a guarantee of future eligibility.

## Always disclose prior declines

On a new application, you will typically be asked whether you have been declined or rated. **Answer truthfully.** Misrepresentation can void a policy later when your family needs it most.
